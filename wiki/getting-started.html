Getting started with ValueApp

This page describes the basic steps from accessing ValueApp to creating your first project and interpreting the calculated planning effort and fees.

1. Access & login

ValueApp is typically provided as a web application for professional use. Depending on your organisation and national context, access may be coordinated by:

  • Your office or group responsible for planning tools
  • A professional association (e.g. OAI, SIA or similar partners)
  • A pilot or research collaboration with ETH Zürich or a r c experts

If you do not yet have access, please reach out to your internal contact person or via the address listed on the main website’s contact section.

2. Creating your first project

Once you can log in, the typical first step is to create a project that represents a real or prototype commission. This allows you to explore the Value Method on realistic data.

  1. Open the project overview screen in ValueApp.
  2. Select “New project” (or similar wording in your version).
  3. Enter a short, descriptive project name (e.g. “Housing, Zürich West, phase 1”).
  4. Specify key parameters such as location, building type and any mandatory metadata required in your national setup.
  5. Save the project and open it for further configuration.

At this stage, the calculated planning effort is not yet final. The value becomes more meaningful once you define the project scope and service profile.

3. Defining scope and services

The ETH Value Method separates project characteristics from the services that planning teams provide. In ValueApp, this is expressed through structured inputs for:

  • Project size and complexity
  • Functional mix (e.g. housing, working, learning, healing)
  • Planning stages / phases
  • Disciplines involved (architecture, civil engineering, building services, etc.)

Completing these inputs allows ValueApp to estimate a planning effort that is both reproducible and comparable across similar projects.

4. Reading the results

After the project and service inputs are defined, ValueApp computes indicative planning effort and, depending on configuration, associated fees. Results are typically presented as:

  • Total planning effort (e.g. in hours or person-days)
  • Effort by discipline and/or phase
  • Optional: fee distribution based on agreed parameters

The key idea is transparency: all assumptions leading to the result are visible and can be discussed, rather than relying on opaque lump-sum metrics.

5. Next steps

Once you are familiar with the basic workflow, typical next steps include:

  • Comparing variants of the same project (e.g. different scopes or phasing)
  • Using ValueApp results as a basis for fee discussions with clients
  • Building internal benchmarks across executed projects

For questions not covered here, refer to the FAQ page or the partners listed on the main website.

Before you begin

Make sure you have at least one real project in mind and, if possible, the typical information you would use in fee or effort discussions.
