Tutorial – step-by-step through the ValueApp

This tutorial walks you through the ValueApp using an example project. The focus is on what to enter in each tab and how to read the results.

Overview

Four tabs, one method

The ValueApp is structured into four tabs: Beschrieb, Projekt, Leistung and Zusammenfassung. They follow the logic of the ETH Value Method from project context to effort and service distribution.

The app connects project description, determinants, adjustment factors and services into one coherent flow.
Step 1

Beschrieb – describe the project

Start with a clear project description. This information appears in reports and provides context when you compare projects later.

  • Enter the project title and address.
  • Set the status (e.g. “In Planung” or “Ausführung”).
  • Describe the starting situation, task and key constraints in 5–10 lines.
  • Define the organisational setup on the planning and contractor side.
Example of the Beschrieb tab filled for an educational building.
Saving and feedback

Use the “Speichern” button in the app to store your progress. The “Feedback” button opens a short form where you can report issues or suggest improvements.

Step 2

Projekt – quantity, uses and complexity

In the Projekt tab you translate the brief into numbers and a complexity assessment.

2.1 Determinant and use mix

Enter the determinating quantity, usually the gross floor area GF. Then assign percentages of this area to the relevant uses such as Wohnen, Arbeiten or Ausbilden und forschen.

Project tab before assigning uses.
All gross floor area assigned to “Ausbilden und forschen” for an educational building.

2.2 Complexity and challenges

Next, assess complexity. You describe challenges in four dimensions (design, technical, spatial, organisational) and position them in the leadership framework.

Description of project-specific challenges and their position in the leadership framework.

The app calculates an adjustment factor based on the centre of gravity of your entries. This factor shifts the expected effort within the prediction interval of the base model.

2.3 Risk level

Finally, set the risk level: no special, increased or high requirements. This reflects project-specific uncertainty and can influence the upper part of the effort range.

Step 3

Leistung – role and hours by phase

In the Leistung tab you define your responsibility and see how the calculated hours distribute across phases.

  • Choose your role (e.g. architect, architect & general planner, general planner).
  • Highlight focus topics such as design, construction management or cost planning.
  • Review the total hours for 100 % basic services.
  • Check the distribution across SIA phases 1–6 and adjust where justified.
Example of roles, focus topics and hours per phase.
Multiple disciplines

For multi-discipline projects (architecture, structural engineering, electrical, heating/ventilation, sanitary), repeat the steps in the Projekt and Leistung tabs for each discipline. The summary shows them side by side.

Step 4

Zusammenfassung – reading the one-pager

The Zusammenfassung tab is read-only. It compiles all relevant information into a structured overview: project description, organisational setup, use mix, effort per m², risk level, hours by phase and, where applicable, additional services.

You can export or print this page and attach it to fee offers, internal planning documents or procurement procedures. It documents why a certain effort level is plausible given the project’s determinants and complexity.
