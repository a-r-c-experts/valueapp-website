Examples

This page shows two worked examples: a simple feasibility or competition project and a more complex educational building. Both follow the same four steps in the ValueApp.

Example 1 – Simple feasibility / competition project

Small residential project, clear task, few stakeholders, low-to-medium complexity.

Step 1 – Beschrieb

The project is a small residential building with approximately 100 m² GF.
The task is to examine feasibility and prepare a concept for a possible building permit or competition entry.
The client is a private owner with a standard schedule and no unusual contractual constraints.

In the Beschrieb tab you:

  • Use a clear project title and enter the address.
  • Set the status to “In Planung”.
  • Describe the task in a short paragraph (existing situation, goal of the study, important constraints).
  • Choose an organisational setup with individual planners and no general contractor.

Step 2 – Projekt

In the Projekt tab you enter a gross floor area of 100 m².
The main use is “Wohnen”, for example:

  • Wohnen – 95% of GF
  • Wohnen 2 – 5% of GF (e.g. ancillary residential space)

Complexity is mostly in the simple to complicated range: standard residential typology, known regulations, no special technical systems.
You position most challenges in the einfach/kompliziert area of the leadership framework and set the risk level to Keine spez. Anforderungen.

Step 3 – Leistung

As an architect you select the role “Architekt/in” and focus topics such as design and early cost planning.
The app calculates a total of roughly 300 hours for 100% basic services,
with a stronger emphasis on phases 2 and 3 (pre-design and design) and fewer hours in execution.

Step 4 – Zusammenfassung

The summary provides a compact overview: project description, organisational setup, use mix, effort per m² and hours by phase.
For a feasibility or competition, this page can be attached to internal planning documents or used as a reference for fee discussions.

What this example shows

For small, relatively simple projects, the ValueApp still uses the same base models and complexity logic. The resulting hours are modest, but fully traceable and can be compared with larger projects.

Example 2 – Educational building (Kindergarten)

Educational building with clear function but higher design, organisational and spatial complexity.

Context and data basis

This example is based on a realised kindergarten project.
The building belongs to the usage group “Lernen / Forschen” and is part of the data basis for the corresponding base model.

Educational and research buildings with prediction intervals for planning effort.

Step 1 – Beschrieb

The Beschrieb tab contains a structured project description: location, client, context
(e.g. existing neighbourhood, outdoor spaces), pedagogical concept and special requirements such as inclusion,
flexibility of spaces or specific safety constraints.

Project description in the ValueApp for the kindergarten example.

Step 2 – Projekt

The determinating quantity is the gross floor area of the kindergarten.
The entire GF is assigned to the use “Ausbilden und forschen”.
This links the project directly to the educational base model.

Project tab with 100% of the gross floor area assigned to “Ausbilden und forschen”.

Complexity is assessed as higher than in the residential example: demanding spatial organisation,
pedagogical concept, integration into an existing context and coordination of several stakeholders.
The challenges are positioned partly in the complex domain of the leadership framework.

Challenges described and positioned in the leadership framework; the centre of gravity leads to an adjustment factor.

Step 3 – Leistung

The architectural office takes the role of “Architekt/in” or “Architekt/in & Generalplaner/in”,
depending on the setup. Focus topics include design, coordination with specialists, construction management and cost planning.

Calculated hours for 100% basic services and their distribution across SIA phases.

The total hours are higher than in the simple example, both because of the larger GF and due to the higher adjustment factor.
Phases 3–5 (projecting, tendering and execution) account for the majority of effort.

Step 4 – Zusammenfassung

The summary shows the kindergarten as a reference project: clearly documented determinants,
complexity assessment and resulting hours. Such summaries can be used as internal benchmarks,
for transparent fee discussions or in public procurement procedures.

What this example shows

Complexity and context matter. Even with the same determinating quantity, the adjustment factor derived from the leadership framework can significantly shift the expected effort. The kindergarten example illustrates how this is transparently documented in the ValueApp.

Data provenance

Realised projects are documented in a separate data and reference system. These data sets feed the statistical base models behind the ValueApp.
