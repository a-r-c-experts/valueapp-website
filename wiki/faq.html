QUESTIONS

Frequently asked questions

This page addresses recurring questions from planning teams, clients and partners using or evaluating ValueApp and the ETH Value Method.

General questions

What is ValueApp in one sentence?

ValueApp is a digital implementation of the ETH Value Method that supports transparent, data-informed estimation of planning effort and fees across building projects.

Who develops and maintains ValueApp?

The method originates from research at ETH Zürich and is applied and further developed in practice by a r c experts together with national and international partners such as OAI and SIA.

Is ValueApp a replacement for national fee regulations?

No. ValueApp does not replace legal or contractual frameworks. It complements them by offering a structured way to discuss planning effort, scope and project complexity using a transparent set of assumptions.

Method & interpretation

Does ValueApp deliver a “correct” fee?

ValueApp does not claim to produce a single “correct” number. Instead, it provides a consistent, reproducible basis for discussion that can be compared across projects and scenarios.

How should I read the planning effort results?

The calculated effort is best understood as an indicative volume that reflects the declared scope and project characteristics. It is particularly useful for:

  • Comparing project variants or scopes
  • Structuring internal resource planning
  • Providing a transparent basis in fee discussions

Can I adjust assumptions to local practice?

National adaptations and partner configurations may include parameters calibrated to local practice. For specific calibration questions, please refer to the contact channels listed on the main website.

Usage & collaboration

Who typically uses ValueApp inside an office?

Typical users include partners and project leaders responsible for fee proposals, resource planning and contract discussions. In larger organisations, specialised calculation or controlling roles might also work with ValueApp.

Can clients see or use ValueApp directly?

In many constellations, results are shared with clients as part of a discussion, while direct access depends on contractual and organisational decisions. The transparency of assumptions is intentionally designed to support such shared discussions.

How does ValueApp handle different disciplines?

The method and software are designed to represent architectural, civil and building services contributions. Depending on the configuration, discipline-specific views and distributions can be explored in more detail.
